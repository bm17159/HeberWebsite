Heber City, located in Wasatch County, Utah, is a charming community with a population of around 16,856 as of the 2020 census. Situated about 43 miles southeast of Salt Lake City, Heber City is nestled in the picturesque Heber Valley, surrounded by the stunning Wasatch Mountains. The city is known for its scenic beauty, outdoor recreational opportunities, and rich cultural heritage

The climate in Heber City features large seasonal temperature differences, with warm to hot summers and cold winters. This creates a perfect environment for a variety of outdoor activities, including skiing, hiking, and mountain biking. The city is also close to several large reservoirs, such as Jordanelle, Deer Creek, and Strawberry, which offer additional recreational opportunities

Heber City has a rich history dating back to its founding in 1859 by Mormon pioneers. The city was named after Heber C. Kimball, a prominent leader in the Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints. Over the years, Heber City has grown into a vibrant community that blends its historical roots with modern amenities, making it a wonderful place to visit or call home
